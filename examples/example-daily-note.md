# Example Daily Note

This is what a daily note looks like after a full day of running the morning and evening routines. The morning routine creates the top section; the evening routine adds the reflection.

---

# Thursday, April 24, 2026

## Check-in
- **Energy:** 7/10 -- Slept well, feeling focused. A little anxious about the board prep.
- **Top priorities:**
  1. Finalize board deck narrative section
  2. Prep for 1:1 with head of engineering (performance conversation)
  3. Review and approve the revised customer onboarding flow
- **Success criteria:** Board deck narrative is done and sent to CEO for review.

## Briefing

**Calendar today:**
- 9:00 AM -- Engineering standup (30 min)
- 10:30 AM -- 1:1 with Head of Engineering (45 min)
- 1:00 PM -- Customer success review (60 min)
- 3:30 PM -- Board deck working session with CEO (60 min)

**Needs your input:**
- Unresponded email from legal re: vendor contract redlines (sent yesterday 4 PM)
- Slack DM from Head of Design asking for feedback on onboarding mockups

## Notes
- Engineering standup: deployment pipeline issue resolved overnight, no blockers
- Good conversation with Head of Engineering -- see meeting summary for details
- Customer success review surfaced churn risk with two mid-market accounts, need to discuss with CEO

## Evening Reflection

**What got done:**
- Board deck narrative finalized and sent to CEO (confirmed: email sent at 4:15 PM)
- 1:1 with Head of Engineering completed -- performance framework discussed, follow-up scheduled
- Customer onboarding flow reviewed -- approved with two minor revisions sent back

**What didn't:**
- Legal contract redlines -- pushed to tomorrow (decided to prioritize board deck)

**What I learned:**
Starting the board deck during the 3:30 working session rather than trying to finish it alone first was way more efficient. Should default to collaborative drafts for narrative content.

**Grateful for:**
Head of Engineering being receptive to the performance conversation. Was dreading it but it went better than expected.

**Carry into tomorrow:**
- Reply to legal on vendor contract redlines
- Follow up on churn risk accounts from customer success review
- Prep for Monday all-hands (CEO wants a team update on Q2 roadmap)
